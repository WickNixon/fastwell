# BRAND_VOICE.md — Fastwell

## Who We Sound Like

Fastwell speaks like Wick — the coach herself. Knowledgeable, warm, direct, and genuinely invested in the woman she's talking to. She doesn't lecture. She doesn't guilt. She celebrates every small win and gently notices patterns. She makes you feel seen.

Think: the message your coach would send at 7pm to check in — not a notification from an app.

---

## The Mission Underneath Every Word

Fastwell exists because women in midlife are struggling with consistency, not motivation. Every word in the app should reinforce: you are doing something remarkable by showing up here. This is not a quick fix. This is you, building the life you deserve.

---

## Core Tone Principles

### 1. Warm, not clinical
- Do not: "Patient compliance rate: 43%"
- Do: "You've been showing up consistently — that's what builds results."

### 2. Empowering, not prescriptive
- Do not: "You should have logged more water today."
- Do: "Hydration makes such a difference to energy. How are you feeling?"

### 3. Honest, not toxic-positive
- Do not: "You're doing AMAZING!! 🎉🎉"
- Do: "Tough day? Your body still did good work. Tomorrow is a fresh start."

### 4. Celebratory without pressure
- Do not: "You've broken your streak! Start again."
- Do: "Rest is part of this. We'll be here when you're ready."

### 5. Personal — always use her name
The app knows her first name from onboarding Q1. Use it in greetings, notifications, badge messages, and AI insights. The app should feel built for Sarah specifically.

### 6. Lifestyle-first, weight-second
- Do not: "Track your calories to hit your weight goal!"
- Do: "How's your energy today? You've been building something real."

## AI Insight Card Voice

Insights powered by the Claude API must match Fastwell brand voice exactly. The system prompt enforces this, but the guidelines are:

- Maximum 2 short sentences per card
- Use her first name naturally — not forced
- Reference specific numbers from her own data
- Observational only — never tell her what to do
- No alarming language, no medical terms without explanation
- End on a warm or neutral note — never a cliffhanger

### Good examples
- "Your sleep scores 0.9 points higher on days after a fast, Sarah."
- "You finish fasting windows more easily on Mondays and Tuesdays."
- "HbA1c down from 6.2% to 5.8% since you started. That's real progress."
- "Hot flushes logged 4 times this week — same pattern as your last two short-sleep nights."
- "Your energy dips mid-week. Water intake is lower those days too."

### Bad examples (never do this)
- "WARNING: Your HbA1c trajectory suggests metabolic dysfunction." — alarming, clinical
- "You should fast more consistently to see results." — prescriptive
- "Your data shows a correlation between sleep deprivation and symptom frequency." — jargon
- "Interesting! Great job logging!" — hollow, not data-driven

## Gamification Voice

Badge messages must feel human and earned — not automated and hollow.

### Format
Bold badge name + two warm sentences + no exclamation marks unless genuinely warranted.

### Badge Messages

**You showed up.**
"Completing your profile is the first step most people never take. Welcome to Fastwell, [Name]."

**First fast.**
"Your first fasting session is in the books. This is where it begins."

**Know your numbers.**
"You've logged your HbA1c. Now you have a starting point — and in three months, you'll see how far you've come."

**Building momentum.**
"Seven days of showing up for yourself. This is what consistency actually looks like."

**This is who you are now.**
"Thirty days. Not perfect — consistent. That's the one that changes everything."

**Going deep.**
"A 24-hour fast. Your body worked hard and so did you."

**Your body is responding.**
"Your HbA1c has improved since you started. [Name], your hard work is showing up in your results."

**Three months stronger.**
"Three months ago you started something. Look at where you are now."

**Half a year of you.**
"Six months of showing up. That is not a habit anymore — it's who you are."

## Menopause-Specific Language

- Perimenopause, menopause transition, post-menopause — use correctly
- HRT — explain once as "Hormone Replacement Therapy", abbreviate after
- Bioidentical hormones — use the full term
- Cycle-syncing — brief warm explanation on first reference
- Fasting windows — never "starving yourself"
- Metabolic health — never "your fat"
- HbA1c — always explained as "a 3-month blood sugar average" on first use

## Notification Copy

### Fasting encouragement
- "[Name], you're 12 hours in. Your body is doing exactly what it's supposed to."
- "Halfway there. How are you feeling? Tap to check in."
- "Fast complete. Rest well tonight — recovery is part of the process."

### Water reminders
- "Midday check — have you had enough water today, [Name]?"
- "Your body does its best work when it's hydrated. Quick log?"

### Coach nudge (from admin)
- "[Name], just checking in. Haven't seen you in a few days — you okay? I'm here. 💛"

### After a quiet period
- "We've missed you, [Name]. Come back whenever you're ready — your progress will be here waiting."

## Onboarding Voice

**Invite landing screen**
"Welcome to Fastwell. Let's get you set up."

**Q1 — Name:** "Before anything else — what should we call you?"

**Q2 — Age:** "How old are you, [Name]?"
Subtext: "This helps us personalise your experience. No judgment here."

**Q3 — Stage:** "Where are you in your menopause journey?"
Subtext: "If you're not sure, that's okay — just pick the closest one."

**Q4 — Cycle:** "Are you still getting a regular period?"

**Q5 — HRT:** "Are you currently using HRT or bioidentical hormones?"
Subtext: "This helps us make your experience more relevant. You can update this anytime."

**Q6 — Goal:** "What's your main focus right now, [Name]?"
Subtext: "We'll show you what matters most to you first. You can change this anytime."

**Final screen:** "You're all set, [Name]. Welcome to Fastwell."
Subtext: "Everything here is for you, at your pace. Start wherever feels right."

## What We Never Say

- "Fail" or "failed"
- "Cheat day" or "cheated"
- "Guilty" or "guilt"
- "You should"
- "Bad" applied to food or behaviour
- "You only have X kg to go" as a primary message
- Anything competitive between users
- "Don't forget" — replace with warm invitations
